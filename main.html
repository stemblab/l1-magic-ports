$\ell_1-\mathrm{MAGIC}$

[http://users.ece.gatech.edu/~justin/l1magic/, $\ell_1-\mathrm{MAGIC}$] is a suite of Matlab software for "solving the convex optimization programs central to compressive sampling". Here we consider ports to other languages. Presently only a stand-in for Matlab's linsolve.m function is provided to allow l1eq_pd.m to run in Octave. As a toy problem, consider solving $Ax=b$, where $$ A= \begin{bmatrix} 1&-1&1\\1&2&4 \end{bmatrix}, \,\,\,\,\,\,\,\,\,\,\,\, b=\begin{bmatrix}1\\4\end{bmatrix}. $$ Obviously we can't invert $A$, but if $x$ is known to be 1-sparse, then $x=[0~0~1]^T$ is a [/?id=b007z,unique solution].
